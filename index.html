Hi, I'm Caitlin.

I am a forward-looking medical librarian at Yale, where I coordinate our general education program and make user-friendly guides, tools and videos that help promote library resources and teach library skills. I teach in the curriculum for the Yale School of Medicine and coordinate our Personal Librarian Program. I'm newish to New England by way of Missouri, Tennessee, and, most recently, California. When I'm not working, you can find me kayaking, hiking, dancing to bluegrass music, or checking out a new brewery. Feel free to reach out or check out my full resume.

Library Blog

The goals of my content on the Cushing/Whitney Medical Library's blog are to: encourage utilization of research resources beyond PubMed and Google Scholar, facilitate finding difficult types of information, promote new resources and classes, and supplement library workshop program content.

Resource Spotlight started as a challenge to educate myself on the vast array of resources made available to the Yale community by the library. The posts, now six times per year, showcase highlights from the collection - strategically targeting expensive, underutilized, or surprising items. While the cause of resource utilization is difficult to track, the posts are well-read by our users. See the whole series here.

The How Do I Find series acknowledges that even the biggest and broadest database like Scopus is limited. Some types of information simply cannot be found in one place, and this series strives to provide a shortlist of resources and strategies for locating types of information such as: conference proceedings, book chapters, clinical guidelines, and more. This series is posted quarterly and authored collaboratively. See the whole series here.

Conference Presentations

Featured: NAHSL 2018

The North Atlantic Health Science Libraries annual meeting took place in October in Manchester, NH. I was able to present both a paper with my colleague Alexandria and a lightning talk. You can read a little about each project and click through to the slides for both presentations below.

New to the profession and new to the region, my colleague Alexandria and I set out to learn about what our positions and library services look like at other academic health centers in the region. We were able to visit schools in three states and gained valuable insight into eight primary areas including library workshops, systematic review services, and more.

Historically, my colleagues had spent an hour with each PA student in December on their thesis projects. This amount of time was unsustainable and often not productive. I designed a self-assessment tool that students took, and then planned research workshops. Each workshop was tailored based on the survey responses of the people in the room and the students had the benefit of peer feedback on their projects.
