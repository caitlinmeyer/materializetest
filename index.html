Orphan Film Symposium

This project involved creating information architecture and content strategy recommendations for the Orphan Film Symposium. I was project lead, and my primary contributions to the final product were user interviews, interpreting that research, content audit, content recommendations, creating the initial Axure wireframe, and assembling the report. Our process started with a three-pronged research approach that consisted of user interviews, a comprehensive peer review and a content survey of the site's past iterations. We were curious about how usable the site was for its audience, what others in the space were doing, and what content the organization already had that it could leverage anew.

Introduction to Zotero

Building an interactive slideshow to teach students about the wonders of Zotero was our first team project at WI+RE. Collaborating on the writing, storyboarding, and creating the included videos with my teammates helped me learn skills in programs such as Camtasia and Videoscribe. The presentation framework, reveal.js, is a nifty tool that enables users to navigate right for the next slide, but down if they need additional information before they feel comfortable proceeding written in simple HTML.

Down to the WI+RE

WI+RE needed a critical look at its records management strategy, and my class project was the perfect opportunity to do so. Four distinct stages of work occurred over the course of this project: record inventory, systems analysis, needs assessment and recommendations. Each of the first three stages revealed key takeaways that were crucial in the ultimate records management strategy, which was presented in a set of modular steps. Check out the final poster!

Determining Functional Requirements

This project involved assembling a functional requirements document for a landscape architecture firm seeking to implement a digital asset management system that accomodates a variety of formats, user types, security levels and more. The goal of the project was to create a document that the client could take to vendor meetings to ensure the product could meet their needs. The process included a series of lengthy interviews with stakeholders in different departments. From there, I prepared a briefing on the client's goals, current archiving and preservation strategy and current digital asset management strategy.

Folk Forum

Folk Forum was created for a project in ETHNO 200, Audiovisual Archives in the 21st Century. We were tasked with coming up with a pitch for the creation of an archive of our choosing and developing everything from collection development policies and preservation and access plans to physical layouts of the space and revenue streams. While a creative project, it involved a hefty amount of research and the experience of producing all of these professional documents was immensely worthwhile. The proposal included the organization mission and vision, a code of ethics, collection scope, acquisition policies, facilities, readiness plans, preservation and access planning, and a preliminary year one budget.
