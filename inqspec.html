Resource Spotlight started as a challenge to educate myself on the vast array of resources made available to the Yale community by the library. The posts, now six times per year, showcase highlights from the collection - strategically targeting expensive, underutilized, or surprising items. While the cause of resource utilization is difficult to track, the posts do average X minutes by our readers.

The How Do I Find series acknowledges that even the biggest and broadest database like Scopus is limited. Some types of information simply cannot be found in one place, and this series strives to provide a shortlist of resources and strategies for locating types of information such as: conference proceedings, book chapters, clinical guidelines, and more. This series is posted quarterly and authored collaboratively.
