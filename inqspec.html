Introducing WI+RE

Writing Instruction & Research Education is a grant-funded, cross campus collaborative team at UCLA making multimedia online learning modules to help undergraduates further develop crucial writing and research skills.

Toolkit

Media: Adobe Suite, Audacity, Camtasia, Hype3, Videoscribe.
Project Management: Box, Github, Google Docs, Trello.

My Role

I worked in every stage of module production (learning objectives, scripting, recording, producing visuals), as well as conducting user testing, learning management system implementation, and promotion.

Sample Projects

Reading Scholarly Articles Draft

This project is still in its refining stages, but our hope was to provide some reading strategies for scholarly articles, ranging from how to effectively skim to how to annotate well. I did the animation, audio editing, and final assemblage.

Finding Scholarly Articles Coming Soon

This project is nearing completion and should be available soon. The goal was to provide a generalized overview of how to navigate scholarly databases to find articles, then allow the students to try it out on a database for their discipline. I created all of the animation for this project.

Literature Review Videos

The intention here was to create an entire instructional sequence for writing literature reviews complete with videos, images, activities, and assessment. I graduated before the project was completed, but here are two of the pieces of the puzzle: "A Literature Review is More than a Summary" and "Creating Literature Reviews".

This video reimagined the visuals for a portion of an NCSU Libraries video, and the animation was done in Videoscribe.
I edited the script, created the visuals, and edited together the final product.

This video was intended to be the introduction to the entire literature review instructional sequence, illustrating to students the usefulness of the genre of writing for a variety of disciplines and situations.
I wrote the script, created the visuals in Hype3, and edited together the final product.

Introduction to Zotero

This project utilized videos, gifs, and the reveal.js framework to create a robust introduction to the citation management software Zotero. My part of this project included determining the information architecture, screencasting for one of the videos, writing copy, and creating gifs. I got to make a poster and present at the 2017 Code4Lib conference on this project.

Research Portal Videos

This was a quick project done for the Undergraduate Research Centers at UCLA, who wanted to teach faculty and students how to use their new undergraduate research portal. I wrote the initial script, did the screencasting, and assembled the final product.
