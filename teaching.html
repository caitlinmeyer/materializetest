Teaching Experience

I taught throughout graduate school as a teaching assistant in the Communication Studies department at UCLA. My favorite class I taught were the discussion sections for Greg Bryant's Communication Theory class. I think it's important to create an active learning environment for my students, involving them in activities instead of defaulting to lecturing. On the first day of each quarter, I would go around and have students introduce themselves and share the ways in which they learn best. In an environment as diverse as UCLA, I also found it important to find a variety of methods to share the same information since different strategies resonate with different types of students. This manifested in quarters full of games, spontaneous public speaking, group work, and more. Student evaluations of my teaching are available upon request.


Google Slides Collaborative Presentations

Inverting the teaching model, I decided to split the class into groups. Each group was given a section of the reading to present and a link to a collaborative Google Slides presentation. From there, they had 20 minutes to figure out a slide to present to the rest of the class and a five minute talk. From a pedagogical standpoint, it was fun to see which information students prioritized on the slides and how they divided up speaking duties. Example 1 | Example 2 | Example 3

Finals Review: March Madness Edition

In honor of the NCAA March Madness basketball tournament, I decided to theme our review session with a bracket challenge. Students were given random seeds and called up to answer questions. Those who answered correctly progressed through the bracket. Slides

Games

Another mainstay of our sections were games built using Kahoot, which lets students answer questions from their phones/computers, and Factile (fka Jeopardy Rocks!), a customizeable Jeopardy board. Students enjoyed these activities because it took the jargon and discipline vernacular of lecture and the readings into understandable terms, did not put pressure/single out individuals, and could be used at home as study tools.

Google Forms Shaping Classes

I offered a standing Google Forms link that allowed students to submit questions for class anonymously ahead of section each week. This let me know where they were at so I could plan accordingly, as well as provided a forum for students to ask questions without embarrassment.
